From Contact-Centre Cost
to a Self-Improving CX

CommBank has already proven AI can run at contact-centre scale. The next horizon: making every layer that touches the customer improve itself — with the digital-to-contact-centre handoff as the signal that proves it's working.

CBA and Microsoft built an orchestration agent that resolves voice and messaging at genuine scale — ~50,000 calls a day, 84.6% of messaging resolved end to end. The calls that still reach a person are the visible tip of a much larger problem.

Every escalation hides a distinct, fixable failure:

  • Bug-driven — a defect in the digital product forced the call. The fix belongs with engineering.
  • Journey-driven — the customer had the right intent, but the digital journey couldn't complete the task.
  • Genuinely unresolved — a later agent version or small journey fix would close it without a human.
  • The hidden cost — for every caller, an unknown multiple hit the same friction, didn't call, and quietly abandoned the channel.
↓ Escalations down, release over release
Escalation rate trend · Amplitude + Wave

Two signals feed one loop. Behavioural data shows where the journey broke and which agent version handled the turn; the specialist who takes the call already knows why it escalated — judgement that today lives in call notes, not the product backlog. Structured together, a spike in a bug-tagged reason code confirms what the behavioural data flagged, giving product teams an evidenced backlog item rather than an anecdote. Amplitude, Statsig, and Wave turn that loop into something that runs — and improves — itself.
